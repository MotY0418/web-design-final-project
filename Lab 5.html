About SDG 4

SDG 4 aims to ensure inclusive, equitable, and quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all. Education is a powerful driver of development and one of the strongest instruments for reducing poverty and improving health, gender equality, peace, and stability.

Targets of SDG 4

  • Free primary and secondary education
  • Equal access to technical/vocational training
  • Eliminate gender disparities in education
  • Universal literacy and numeracy
  • Build and upgrade inclusive schools
  • Increase qualified teachers

Key Statistics

Global Education Facts

  • 258 million children and youth were out of school in 2018
  • 773 million adults remain illiterate (two-thirds are women)
  • Only 40% of girls in sub-Saharan Africa complete lower secondary school
  • 1 in 5 children affected by conflict are out of school

Digital Classrooms for Rural Areas

This initiative provides solar-powered digital education kits to underserved schools in rural communities, enhancing access to modern education tools. The program has already reached over 500 schools across 20 developing nations.

A global network connecting qualified educators with communities in need, providing training and resources to improve teaching quality worldwide.
