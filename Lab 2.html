Goal 4: Quality Education

Sustainable Development Goal 4 aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.

This goal recognizes that education is essential for escaping poverty, improving health outcomes, achieving gender equality, and fostering peaceful societies.

Key Targets

  • Ensure all children complete free, equitable primary and secondary education
  • Provide equal access to affordable vocational training
  • Eliminate gender disparities in education
  • Ensure universal literacy and numeracy
  • Build and upgrade inclusive schools
  • Substantially increase the supply of qualified teachers
  • Expand global scholarships for developing countries

Why It Matters

Education transforms lives and is at the heart of the UN's Sustainable Development Goals:

  • Reduces poverty (SDG 1)
  • Improves health outcomes (SDG 3)
  • Promotes gender equality (SDG 5)
  • Fosters economic growth (SDG 8)
  • Reduces inequalities (SDG 10)
  • Promotes peaceful societies (SDG 16)

Despite progress, 258 million children and youth were out of school in 2018, and 773 million adults remain illiterate.
